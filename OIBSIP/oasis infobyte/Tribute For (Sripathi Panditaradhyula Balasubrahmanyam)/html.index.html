Sripathi Panditaradhyula Balasubrahmanyam

Yongerest Dynamic Indian Playback Singer

Sripathi Panditaradhyula Balasubrahmanyam (4 June 1946 /25 September 2020), also referred to as SPB or SP Balu or Balu, was an Indian playback singer, television presenter, actor, music composer, dubbing artist, and film producer.

Few Lines About S.P.Subrahmanyam

  • S.P.Balasubrahmanyam was Born in 1946 into a middle class family from Nellore in Andhra Pradesh.
  • He is widely regarded as one of the greatest Indian singers of all time.
  • He predominently worked in Telugu,Tamil,Kannada,Malayalam,and Hindi films and sang in a total of 16 languages
  • He was a recipient of the Padma Shri(2001),Padma Bhushan (2011), and Padma Vibhushan(posthumously)(2021)from the Government of India.
  • His father, S. P. Sambamurthy, was a Harikatha artist who also acted in plays.
  • His mother was Sakunthalamma, who died on 4 February 2019.
  • In 1964, he won the first prize in a music competition for amateur singers organized by the Madras-based Telugu Cultural Organization.
  • Balasubrahmanyam accidentally became a dubbing artist with K. Balachander's film Manmadha Leela (1976).
  • Renowned Indian singer SP Balasubrahmanyam, a Guinness world record holder for his more than 40,000 songs over 50 years, has died aged 74.
  • On 25 September 2020, Balasubrahmanyam died in Chennai after being hospitalized for over a month for complications due to COVID-19.
  • In the 1970s, he also worked with M. S. Viswanathan in Tamil movies for actors such as M. G. Ramachandran, Sivaji Ganesan and Gemini Ganesan.
  • Balasubrahmanyam received two of India's top civilian honours, Padma Shri in 2001 and Padma Bhushan in 2011.
  • Besides being an iconic singer, S P Balasubrahmanyam was also an actor, a producer and a voice actor. He was a recipient of the Padma Shri and the Padma Bhushan, and a winner of six National Awards. S P Balasubrahmanyam had over 40,000 songs in 16 languages to his credit.
